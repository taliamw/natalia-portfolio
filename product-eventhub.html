Event Hub

Project Overview

EventHub is a centralized campus events platform designed to make discovery, management, and attendance seamless for students and organizers. The home screen prioritizes event discovery with searchable cards and filters. Organizers can create and manage events through a simple dashboard while students can sort and discover events by date, organizer, price, or category.

Problem

Campus events are often scattered across multiple club pages, noticeboards, and messaging groups. Students miss relevant events or struggle to find accurate details. Organizers face friction in publishing events and tracking attendance. This fragmentation reduces participation and complicates coordination.

Research and Insights

User interviews with students and club officers showed a need for a single source of truth, quick filtering, and predictable ticketing flows. Students prioritized discoverability and clear event details. Organizers valued easy event creation, attendee lists, and simple ticket management. These insights shaped a concise information hierarchy and interaction flow.

Solution and Design Approach

EventHub centralizes event data and offers a discovery-first homepage with robust filters for date, organizer, price, and category. Event creation is streamlined with templates for clubs and departments. The design emphasizes scannability, quick RSVP/ticket purchase, and clear status updates. We validated flows through low-fidelity prototypes and iterated based on usability tests with student groups.

Reflection and Next Steps

Building EventHub highlighted the value of reducing cognitive load for students when discovering campus activities. Next steps include improving organizer analytics, adding calendar integrations, and piloting payment and ticketing with a few clubs to validate end-to-end logistics.

Outcome and Impact

As an MVP, EventHub demonstrates improved event visibility and easier management for organizers. The prototype shows potential to increase attendance and simplify student engagement when integrated with campus systems.
