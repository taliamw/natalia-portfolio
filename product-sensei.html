SAUTI

Project Overview

SAUTI is a communication solution designed to bridge the gap between speech- and hearing-impaired individuals and the people around them.

Built as part of the Hack4PWD Hackathon at Microsoft ADC 2024, where it emerged as the winning project, SAUTI combines accessibility-focused product design with modern technology to make communication seamless and inclusive.

I believe the best products don’t just solve problems, they give people possibilities they never had before.


Problem

Communication barriers severely limit independence, access, and participation for people with speech and hearing impairments. Existing solutions are often expensive, unintuitive, or lack support for real-time interactions in everyday scenarios.

Research and Insights

Grounding SAUTI in real needs was essential, so I worked closely with my team to understand the lived experiences of speech- and hearing-impaired users. During our research, a teammate visited Machakos School for the Deaf and encountered firsthand the communication challenges faced without sign language knowledge.

Conversations with instructors revealed deeper accessibility barriers and everyday pain points. To complement these insights, I’m also using user surveys to capture broader perspectives, validate assumptions, and guide our design decisions.

Reflection and next steps

Designing SAUTI taught me the value of deeply understanding user context before building. Our next steps include integrating more languages, refining gesture recognition with ML models, and testing with a broader user group.

Outcome and Impact

SAUTI won 1st place at the Hack4PWD Hackathon 2024 (Microsoft ADC) for its inclusive design, technical execution, and real-world potential. The project received positive feedback from accessibility advocates and is being considered for further development with support from disability-focused organizations.
