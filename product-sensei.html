SENSEI

Project Overview

Sensei is an AI-powered mobile application designed to proactively assist neurodivergent users in identifying and managing sensory overload. Unlike reactive methods that only respond to overstimulation after it occurs, Sensei leverages real-time environmental and user input data to provide personalized alerts and recommendations. The application combines accessibility-focused UI design with advanced detection algorithms to create a user-centered, inclusive experience.

Technology and design should proactively support users’ needs, not just react to them.

Problem

Neurodivergent individuals often face sensory challenges in everyday environments, including crowded spaces, bright lights, or loud sounds. Existing solutions are typically reactive, lack real-time monitoring, and do not provide proactive guidance for managing sensory overload. This gap can negatively affect focus, emotional well-being, and overall quality of life.

Solution and Design Approach

The Sensei app provides a low-sensory interface with customizable alert thresholds and feedback based on environmental cues and user data. I conducted extensive user research and collaborated with neurodivergent individuals and caregivers to map common triggers and scenarios. The design prioritizes simplicity, accessibility, and minimal cognitive load, while the backend leverages Flutter, Dart, and Firebase for smooth cross-platform performance. Iterative testing helped refine notifications, visual cues, and interaction flows to ensure the app was intuitive and reliable.

Reflection and Next Steps

Working on Sensei reinforced the importance of grounding product design in real user needs. Iterative testing, accessibility improvements, and feedback loops remain central to our approach. The next development phases include expanding customization options, improving detection accuracy, and integrating additional support features to further enhance user autonomy and comfort.

Outcome and Impact

Sensei is a proof-of-concept project that aims improve daily experiences for neurodivergent users by providing actionable insights and alerts to prevent sensory overload. It demonstrates how thoughtful, inclusive design combined with proactive technology can deliver measurable positive outcomes for users. The project continues to evolve with ongoing testing and feedback.
