Ngozi UI

Project Overview

NgoziUI is a prototype built for an ML class that enables accessible detection of common skin conditions by uploading or photographing the affected area. The design focuses on clear guidance, straightforward image input, and an easily interpretable results screen so non-expert users can understand next steps.

Problem

Many users lack quick access to dermatology advice. Early signs of skin conditions are often missed or dismissed due to cost or distance from care. Users need a simple, trustworthy way to get preliminary feedback and know whether to seek clinical support.

Research and Insights

Research emphasized trust, clarity, and minimal friction. Users wanted explicit guidance on how to take photos, short explanations of what results mean, and clear disclaimers about the prototype's limits. These insights guided the UI choices and the content shown alongside model output.

Solution and Design Approach

The prototype provides a step-by-step image capture flow, on-screen tips for lighting and framing, and a results screen that presents classification with a confidence indicator and suggested next actions. The interface was iterated using usability tests with peers and instructors to ensure the flow is simple and informative for non-specialists.

Reflection and Next Steps

The prototype validated that accessible design improves user confidence when interacting with ML tools. Next steps include expanding the labeled dataset, improving model calibration, adding explicit privacy controls, and conducting clinical validation before any public release.

Outcome and Impact

As a class prototype, NgoziUI demonstrated how a focused UX can make ML outputs more usable and trustworthy. It serves as a foundation for further research into dataset quality, model robustness, and responsible deployment in low-resource contexts.
